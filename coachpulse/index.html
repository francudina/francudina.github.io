AI Coaching Data Field for Garmin

You lace up, start the activity, and run. Fifteen minutes in, your heart rate is creeping higher than it should. You push through anyway. You finish tired, a little overtrained, not quite sure if today helped or hurt. CoachPulse is the coach you wish had been watching.

The problem

Training alone is hard

A good coach notices when your form slips around kilometre four. They know when to push you and when to pull back, adjusting based on how you look today. Your Garmin is full of biometric data, but none of it talks back. CoachPulse connects that data to an AI and turns it into live coaching cues right on your watch screen while you are still moving.

Under the hood

How it works

CoachPulse runs as a data field inside any Garmin activity. When something notable happens, it packages your current metrics and sends a prompt to the AI model of your choice. The cue comes back and appears on screen. No app switch, no phone unlock. You bring your own API key from OpenAI, Anthropic Claude, or Google Gemini.

When it speaks up

What triggers a coaching cue

CoachPulse fires at meaningful moments: workout start (readiness check), kilometre and time milestones, sustained high effort (zone 3+ for too long), and pace drops below your session average. A periodic check-in interval is also available. Every trigger can be turned on or off individually.

Your intent, in the prompt

Your session, your goal

Set a short session goal before you head out: "easy recovery run", "tempo intervals, stay in zone 3", "long slow distance". That goal travels with every prompt CoachPulse sends. A cue during a recovery run sounds different from one during a race-pace effort, even if the biometrics look similar.

Tone and sensitivity

Coach it the way you want to be coached

Choose a Coaching Style from Relaxed to Aggressive. Control how much the AI weighs fatigue signals with Recovery Focus. Set Response Length to Short, Normal, or Long as your baseline preference. The word count also adapts automatically to the actual size of the data field slot, so responses fit cleanly whether you're on a full-screen layout or a compact split.

Knowing when to stay quiet

Smart enough to leave you alone

After a cue fires, CoachPulse enters a cooldown. No new cues until the timer expires. The default is 5 minutes. A coach who speaks every 30 seconds stops being a coach and starts being a distraction. The last cue stays on screen until replaced, so you never miss it mid-stride.

Getting started

Using CoachPulse on your watch

CoachPulse is a data field, not a standalone app. You add it to a data screen inside an existing activity profile.

Setup steps

1
Install CoachPulse from the Garmin Connect IQ store onto your watch.
2
Open the Garmin Connect app on your phone, navigate to your watch settings, and under Activities and Apps edit a data screen for your chosen activity.
3
Add a data field slot and select CoachPulse. A full-screen single field gives the most room for longer coaching cues.
4
Open CoachPulse settings in the Connect IQ section of the Garmin Connect app. Enter your API key, pick your provider and model, and configure your preferences.
5
Start your activity and swipe to the CoachPulse screen. It shows Monitoring... while idle and cues appear automatically when triggered.

What the screen shows: idle shows "Monitoring..." in white, fetching shows "Preparing..." in grey, a cue appears centred in white, errors in orange. If the API key is missing a yellow prompt reminds you to set it.

Configuration

All settings, explained

All settings are configured through the Garmin Connect IQ app on your phone. Changes take effect the next time you start an activity.

Connection
AI Provider
Which AI service to use: OpenAI, Anthropic Claude, or Google Gemini. Default is OpenAI.
AI Model
The specific model to call. OpenAI: GPT-5.5 Pro, GPT-5.4 mini (default), GPT-5.4 nano. Claude: Opus 4.7, Sonnet 4.6, Haiku 4.6. Gemini: 2.5 Pro, 2.5 Flash (default), 2.5 Flash Lite.
API Key
Your API key for the selected provider. Stored on device only, used exclusively for outbound coaching requests during a workout.
Session
Session Goal
A short description of what you are trying to accomplish today, up to 120 characters. Leave blank to coach based on biometrics and sport type alone.
Session Memory
When on, the AI remembers its recent responses throughout the activity and uses them to avoid repetition and build on prior coaching. OpenAI uses server-side conversation threading; Claude and Gemini receive the last N responses in the system prompt. On by default.
History Size
How many past coaching responses to include in the session memory context for Claude and Gemini. Range 3 to 20. Default 10. Has no effect when Session Memory is off or when using OpenAI (which uses server-side threading).
Coaching Behaviour
Coaching Style
How direct the AI should be. 1 Relaxed to 5 Aggressive. Default is 3 Balanced.
Recovery Focus
How much the AI weighs fatigue signals like body battery and stress. 1 Ignore to 5 Maximum. Default is 3 Balanced.
Response Length
Short: 5 to 6 words. Normal: 5 to 9 words (default). Long: 12 to 25 words. The actual word count also adapts automatically to the data field dimensions, so the same setting works well across full-screen and split-slot layouts.
Trigger Control
Cooldown
Minimum seconds between any two cues. Range 15 to 600. Default 300 seconds (5 minutes).
Check-in Interval
Periodic cue every N minutes regardless of other triggers. Range 0 to 60. Default 0 (off).
Intro Cue
Fire a cue at workout start based on your readiness and session goal. On by default.
Milestone Cues
Fire cues at kilometre and time checkpoints. On by default.
Effort Check-in
Fire a cue when you sustain zone 3+ for longer than the Effort Threshold. On by default.
Effort Threshold
Minutes of continuous zone 3+ effort before the effort check-in fires. Range 5 to 60. Default 20 minutes.
Pace Drop Alert
Fire a cue when your pace drops noticeably below your session average. On by default.
HR Alerts
Fire cues on HR zone breaches (zone 4+ or unexpectedly low) and sudden HR spikes. On by default.

Supported watches

CoachPulse works on Garmin devices that support Connect IQ data fields with the Communications permission, covering most Fenix, Forerunner, Epix, Venu, Marq, and Instinct models from the last several years. A phone with Garmin Connect is required to relay AI requests during a workout.
